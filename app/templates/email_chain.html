Having been gone for some amount of time, Mark Jumper returns one day to find that Dey Company/Dey Industries has much changed.
Bob Dey is dead and entombed in cement on the top floors of the building. The BDTEM offices are empty and languishing.
Even the midnight Fears and Superstitions have abandoned their late-night hallway lurkings.

There is no longer a place for Mark Jumper at DC/DI.

For this he is glad. "Goodbye," is all he plans to say. His official resignation, so at last he can resign himself to resting peace.
But a serendipitous meeting in the hall asks him for a small favour of information. "Will you, please, help us fill in a gap or two in the BDTEM timeline?" asks the Dept of Archives.
Reluctantly he relents and gives what he can, only to find that he is dissatisfied with the completeness of his answers, and the shallowness of their questions.
"Do they even know what gaps they have and what gaps they do not?" He'd rather not think about it.
But, there on the 12th floor, in the Dept. of Depts., dissolving the BDTEM Project by stokes of the pen, a week's worth of paperwork, he fathoms an opportunity to fill in the gaps unfilled, and to nudge open a few new gaps where surely gaps should be.
